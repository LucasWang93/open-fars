Title: Coexact $1$-form spectral gaps of hyperbolic rational homology spheres

Abstract: We discuss a construction of families of hyperbolic rational homology spheres with coexact $1$-form spectral gap uniformly bounded below which is well-suited for explicit computations. Using this, we provide several disjoint intervals containing a limit point of such spectral gaps, the rightmost of which is $[0.8196,0.8277]$. Furthermore, we also exhibit a family of arithmetic examples, answering a question of Abdurrahman-Adve-Giri-Lowe-Zung.

Introduction The coexact 1 1 -form spectrum of hyperbolic three-manifolds has recently sparked much interest. It relates closely to a conjecture of Bergeron and Venkatesh growth of torsion homology in congruence covers [ BV13 ] . In this connection, a key role is played by the first eigenvalue λ 1 ∗ \lambda_{1}^{*} , the coexact 1 1 -form spectral gap . This gap behaves qualitatively very differently than the more classically studied spectral gap on functions λ 1 \lambda_{1} ; a key difference is that the bottom of their L 2 L^{2} -spectra on hyperbolic space ℍ 3 \mathbb{H}^{3} are λ 1 ∗ ​ ( ℍ 3 ) = 0 \lambda_{1}^{*}(\mathbb{H}^{3})=0 and λ 1 ​ ( ℍ 3 ) = 1 \lambda_{1}(\mathbb{H}^{3})=1 respectively. Geometrically, λ 1 \lambda_{1} can be understood in terms of the Cheeger constant [ Cha84 ] , while λ 1 ∗ \lambda_{1}^{*} can be bounded below in terms of suitable stable isoperimetric ratios [ LS18 , Rud23 ] . In [ AAG + 24 ] , the authors initiate a systematic study of the set of spectral gaps of hyperbolic rational homology spheres, i.e. closed hyperbolic three-manifolds with b 1 ​ ( Y ) = 0 b_{1}(Y)=0 , in the spirit of the theory of bass notes for spectra of locally uniform geometries [ Sar23 ] . In particular, they construct infinite families with a uniform lower bound on λ 1 ∗ \lambda_{1}^{*} and study in detail their attendant geometric and topological properties. In this paper, we introduce an alternate construction of infinite families of hyperbolic rational homology spheres with λ 1 ∗ \lambda_{1}^{*} uniformly bounded below. Roughly speaking, our construction is based on a fixed mapping torus M φ M_{\varphi} (of a diffeomorphism φ \varphi of a surface of genus at least two with specific properties) admitting an involution of a special type endowing the cyclic covers of M φ M_{\varphi} with dihedral symmetry. The manifolds we consider are obtained from these cyclic covers by taking the quotient by a fixed-point-free involution. This construction lends itself well to explicit computations in hyperbolic geometry and to direct searches for examples. In fact, our construction yields families for which the sequence of spectral gaps λ 1 ∗ \lambda_{1}^{*} converges, and for which we can provide an explicit arbitrarily small interval containing the limit. The example for which the limit is the largest among those we found leads to the following. Theorem 1 . There exists an infinite family { Y n } \{Y_{n}\} of hyperbolic rational homology spheres such that lim n → ∞ λ 1 ∗ ​ ( Y n ) ∈ [ 0.8196 , 0.8277 ] \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\lambda_{1}^{*}(Y_{n})\in[0.8196,0.8277] . See also Table 1 at the end of the paper for other intervals, disjoint from this one, each containing a limit point. For context, in [ LL22b ] the authors adapted the ideas of [ BS07 ] to study the coexact 1 1 -form spectrum via the Selberg trace formula. In particular, for a given hyperbolic rational homology sphere Y Y , one can obtain explicit information about λ 1 ∗ ​ ( Y ) \lambda_{1}^{*}(Y) taking as input its volume and the complex length spectrum up to a given cutoff (as computed for example using SnapPy [ CDGW ] ). Of course, this strategy cannot be applied to a general infinite family of manifolds. The key point behind Theorem 1 is relating the spectral theory of the manifolds Y n Y_{n} simultaneously to that of a single mapping torus M φ M_{\varphi} , which may be probed by a single (possibly very long) finite computation. Arithmeticity We also positively answer Question 2 in [ AAG + 24 ] , which was motivated by an analogous result regarding the spinor spectrum of hyperbolic surfaces proved in [ AG25 ] . Theorem 2 . There exists an infinite family of arithmetic hyperbolic rational homology spheres with a uniform lower bound on λ 1 ∗ \lambda_{1}^{*} . The family from Theorem 2 consists of arithmetic three-manifolds all commensurable to each other, and almost all of them are not congruence. These are necessary conditions for a family of arithmetic rational homology spheres to admit a uniform gap for λ 1 ∗ . \lambda_{1}^{*}. Indeed, an infinite sequence of arithmetic three-manifolds Benjamini-Schramm converges to ℍ 3 \mathbb{H}^{3} provided that either they are all congruence or they each belong to a different commensurability class [ FR19 ] . Relation to Floer theory The authors’ original interest in explicitly understanding the coexact 1 1 -form spectral gap of hyperbolic rational homology spheres in [ LL22b ] arose from its relation with Floer theoretic invariants of three-manifolds. In particular, it is shown that if λ 1 ∗ ​ ( Y ) 2 \lambda_{1}^{*}(Y) 2 , then Y Y is an L L -space i.e. it has trivial reduced monopole Floer homology [ KM07 ] . In fact, the construction we present in this paper is the higher genus analogue of the one studied in detail in [ Lin20 ] for genus one. In the latter paper, the relevant Thurston geometry is Solv \mathrm{Solv} [ Mar ] , which can be analyzed by hand using ideas from Fourier analysis on solvable groups, providing a geometric proof that all Solv \mathrm{Solv} rational homology spheres are L L -spaces (first proved in [ BGW13 ] via topological methods). Having the relation to Floer theory in mind, and considering that such a simple construction already provides families with significant uniform gap, we conjecture: Conjecture . There exists infinitely many hyperbolic rational homology spheres with λ 1 ∗ 2 \lambda_{1}^{*} 2 . It is not clear whether one can hope for a positive solution to the conjecture by a sequence in a fixed commesurability class. While there are several ways to generalize the construction we present - in particular, the starting point does not need to be a fibered manifold - explicitly understanding λ 1 ∗ \lambda_{1}^{*} for families of manifolds not commensurate to each other lies beyond the reach of our strategy.
